A complete guide to india's most exciting,chaotic and beloved city

Introduction

Bangalore is a city of beautiful contradictions. On one street you will find a 400-year-old temple, and on the very next street a glass-fronted startup office worth a billion dollars. It is a city where retired professors share autorickshaws with software engineers, where the smell of jasmine from a roadside vendor mixes with the aroma of freshly brewed filter coffee drifting out of a Darshini hotel at 7 in the morning.

Known globally as the Silicon Valley of India, Bangalore is home to the country's largest concentration of technology companies, research institutions, and startups. But long before the laptops and the lattes, this city was called the Garden City of India — a name it earned through its extraordinary collection of parks, tree-lined avenues, and a climate so pleasant that the British chose it as their preferred cantonment in all of South India.

Today, Bangalore is the capital of the state of Karnataka and the third most populous city in India. It is governed by the BBMP (Bruhat Bengaluru Mahanagara Palike) for civic administration, served by BMTC (Bangalore Metropolitan Transport Corporation) for buses, and connected underground by BMRC (Bangalore Metro Rail Corporation) through the ever-expanding Namma Metro network.

As the novelist Anita Nair once wrote — "Bangalore is not a city. It is a state of mind." Once you live here, you will understand exactly what she meant.
